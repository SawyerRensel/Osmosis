# Science Study Cards

## Biology

```osmosis
id: bio-cell-parts

Name the major organelles of a eukaryotic cell and their functions:
***
- **Nucleus** — contains DNA, controls cell activities
- **Mitochondria** — ATP production (cellular respiration)
- **Endoplasmic Reticulum**
  - Rough ER — protein synthesis (has ribosomes)
  - Smooth ER — lipid synthesis
- **Golgi Apparatus** — packages and ships proteins
- **Lysosomes** — digest waste and foreign material
- **Cell Membrane** — controls what enters and exits
```

## Physics

```osmosis
id: phys-newton

State Newton's three laws of motion:
***
1. **First Law** (Inertia): An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion at constant velocity, unless acted upon by a net external force.

2. **Second Law**: The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force and inversely proportional to its mass.
   > **F = ma**

3. **Third Law**: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
```

```osmosis
id: phys-em-spectrum

Order the electromagnetic spectrum from longest to shortest wavelength:
***
1. Radio waves (> 1 m)
2. Microwaves (1 mm – 1 m)
3. Infrared (700 nm – 1 mm)
4. **Visible light** (400 – 700 nm)
   - Red → Orange → Yellow → Green → Blue → Violet
5. Ultraviolet (10 – 400 nm)
6. X-rays (0.01 – 10 nm)
7. Gamma rays (< 0.01 nm)
```
