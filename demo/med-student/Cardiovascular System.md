# Cardiovascular System

The cardiovascular system is a closed circulatory system responsible for transporting blood, nutrients, oxygen, and waste products throughout the body. The heart serves as the central pump, beating approximately 100,000 times per day.

![Diagram of the human heart](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Diagram_of_the_human_heart_%28cropped%29.svg/800px-Diagram_of_the_human_heart_%28cropped%29.svg.png)

## Heart Chambers

- **Right Atrium**
    - Receives deoxygenated blood from the body
    - Superior vena cava (upper body)
    - Inferior vena cava (lower body)
    - Coronary sinus (heart muscle drainage)
    - Thin-walled, low-pressure chamber
- **Right Ventricle**
    - Pumps blood to the lungs via the pulmonary trunk
    - Trabeculated inner surface (trabeculae carneae)
    - Moderator band carries conduction fibers
    - Thinner wall than left ventricle (~3-5 mm)
- **Left Atrium**
    - Receives oxygenated blood from the lungs
    - Four pulmonary veins (two from each lung)
    - Smooth-walled posterior portion
    - Left atrial appendage (common site of thrombus in atrial fibrillation)
- **Left Ventricle**
    - Pumps oxygenated blood to the entire body
    - Thickest myocardial wall (~13-15 mm)
    - Conical shape, forms the cardiac apex
    - Generates systemic blood pressure

## Heart Valves

- **Atrioventricular (AV) Valves**
    - Tricuspid valve (right side)
        - Three cusps: anterior, posterior, septal
        - Chordae tendineae attach to papillary muscles
        - Prevents backflow into right atrium
    - Mitral valve (left side)
        - Two cusps: anterior and posterior (bicuspid)
        - Stronger chordae tendineae
        - Most commonly affected valve in rheumatic heart disease
- **Semilunar Valves**
    - Pulmonary valve
        - Three semilunar cusps
        - Between right ventricle and pulmonary trunk
        - Lowest pressure semilunar valve
    - Aortic valve
        - Three semilunar cusps (left, right, posterior)
        - Coronary arteries originate just above left and right cusps
        - Most commonly replaced valve

## Heart Wall Layers

- **Endocardium**
    - Innermost layer lining the chambers
    - Simple squamous epithelium (endothelium)
    - Continuous with the endothelium of blood vessels
    - Subendocardial layer contains Purkinje fibers
- **Myocardium**
    - Thickest layer — the cardiac muscle
    - Composed of cardiac myocytes joined by intercalated discs
    - Intercalated discs contain:
        - Gap junctions (electrical coupling)
        - Desmosomes (mechanical adhesion)
        - Fascia adherens (anchoring)
    - Rich capillary network (one capillary per myocyte)
- **Epicardium** (visceral pericardium)
    - Outermost layer of the heart wall
    - Serous membrane with mesothelial cells
    - Contains coronary arteries and veins
    - Adipose tissue in sulci and grooves

## Arterial System

- Aorta → large elastic arteries
- Muscular arteries → resistance vessels
- Arterioles → primary regulators of blood pressure
- Capillaries → exchange vessels (nutrient/gas exchange)

## Venous System

- Venules → collect from capillary beds
- Small veins → contain valves to prevent backflow
- Large veins → capacitance vessels (hold ~60% of blood volume)
- Vena cavae → return to right atrium

## Coronary Circulation

- **Left coronary artery (LCA)**
    - Left anterior descending (LAD) — "widow maker"
        - Supplies anterior wall of left ventricle
        - Anterior interventricular septum
    - Left circumflex (LCx)
        - Supplies lateral wall of left ventricle
        - Left atrium
- **Right coronary artery (RCA)**
    - Supplies right atrium and right ventricle
    - SA node (in ~60% of people)
    - AV node (in ~80% of people)
    - Posterior descending artery (in right-dominant circulation)
