# Worldbuilding Checklist

A comprehensive framework for building fictional worlds that feel lived-in. Not every story needs every element — but knowing what you've *chosen* to leave out is as important as what you include.

[Worldbuilding — Brandon Sanderson BYU Lecture](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ATNvOk5rIJA)

## Geography & Environment

The physical world shapes everything: culture, conflict, trade, and survival.

### Terrain & Climate

- [ ] Define major landmasses and bodies of water
- [ ] Establish climate zones and weather patterns
  - Tropical, temperate, arid, polar
  - Seasonal variations and extreme weather
  - How climate affects daily life
- [ ] Create natural barriers
  - Mountain ranges that divide cultures
  - Rivers that serve as trade routes
  - Deserts and forests as frontiers
- [ ] Map key resources
  - Fertile land and fresh water
  - Mineral deposits and rare materials
  - Magical resources (if applicable)

### Flora & Fauna

- [ ] Define the ecosystem
  - Keystone species that shape the environment
  - Predator-prey relationships
  - Domesticated animals and their uses
- [ ] Unique creatures
  - What fills ecological niches?
  - How do people interact with wildlife?
  - Dangerous fauna that shapes travel and settlement
- [ ] Plant life
  - Staple crops and agriculture
  - Medicinal plants and poisons
  - Magical or unusual flora

## Culture & Society

Culture emerges from geography, history, and the stories people tell themselves.

### Social Structure

- [ ] Define class systems
  - Nobility, merchants, laborers, outcasts
  - Social mobility — how rigid are the boundaries?
  - How is status displayed?
    - Clothing, speech, mannerisms
    - Where people live
    - Who they can marry
- [ ] Family structures
  - Nuclear, extended, clan-based, chosen
  - Inheritance customs
  - Gender roles and expectations
    - Are they rigid or fluid?
    - How do they vary by class?
    - Recent changes or tensions

### Language & Communication

- [ ] Number of languages
  - Trade languages vs. regional dialects
  - Dead languages with cultural significance
- [ ] Writing systems
  - Who is literate?
  - How is information preserved?
- [ ] Non-verbal communication
  - Gestures, colors, symbols with cultural meaning
  - Taboo words or topics

### Arts & Entertainment

- [ ] Storytelling traditions
  - Oral histories, written literature, performance
- [ ] Music and dance
  - Instruments, styles, occasions
- [ ] Visual arts
  - Preferred mediums, subjects, patrons

## Magic Systems

If your world has magic, it needs rules — even if the reader doesn't know all of them.

### Fundamental Rules

- [ ] Source of magic
  - Where does it come from?
    - Internal (innate talent)
    - External (ambient energy, divine gift)
    - Material (crystals, herbs, blood)
  - Can it be depleted?
- [ ] Limitations and costs
  - Physical toll on the user
  - Material components required
  - Time, training, or ritual needed
  - What magic absolutely *cannot* do
- [ ] Who can use it
  - Innate ability vs. learned skill
  - Hereditary, random, or chosen
  - Social attitudes toward magic users

### Magic in Society

- [ ] Legal status of magic
  - Regulated, forbidden, or revered?
  - Enforcement mechanisms
- [ ] Economic impact
  - Does magic replace technology?
  - Magic-based industries and trades
  - Magical inequality — who benefits?
- [ ] Military applications
  - Battle magic and its limits
  - Magical arms races
  - Defense against magic

## Politics & Power

### Government Structure

- [ ] Form of government
  - Monarchy, republic, theocracy, anarchy
  - How power is transferred
  - Checks on power (or lack thereof)
- [ ] Key institutions
  - Military, judiciary, religious authority
  - Guilds, academies, secret societies
- [ ] Foreign relations
  - Allies, enemies, trade partners
  - Treaties and their fragility
  - Border disputes and territorial ambitions

### Economy & Trade

- [ ] Currency and exchange
  - What backs the currency?
  - Barter vs. monetary systems
  - Banking and debt
- [ ] Key industries
  - Agriculture, mining, crafting, magic
  - Trade routes and their dangers
  - Monopolies and trade wars
- [ ] Wealth distribution
  - Who has the most? Who has the least?
  - Economic tensions driving the plot

## Religion & Belief

- [ ] Pantheon or belief system
  - Monotheistic, polytheistic, animistic, philosophical
  - Active gods vs. distant creators
  - Competing religions and syncretism
- [ ] Religious practices
  - Rituals, holy days, pilgrimages
  - Sacred sites and relics
  - Clergy and their role in society
- [ ] Relationship to magic
  - Is magic divine? Heretical? Separate?
  - Religious orders with magical abilities
  - Conflicts between faith and arcane practice
