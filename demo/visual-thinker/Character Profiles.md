# Character Profiles

Strong characters are the engine of great fiction. A compelling character can carry a weak plot; a strong plot cannot save a flat character. These profiles demonstrate how to build characters that feel alive on the page.

## Character Development Principles

### The Character Triangle

Every memorable character exists at the intersection of three forces:

- **Want** (external goal): What the character pursues consciously
  - Must be concrete and visible to the reader
  - Drives the plot forward
  - Often at odds with what they actually need
- **Need** (internal growth): What the character must learn or become
  - Usually invisible to the character at the start
  - Revealed through conflict and failure
  - The real story lives here
- **Flaw** (the obstacle within): What prevents them from getting what they need
  - Connected to a wound or formative experience
  - Creates internal conflict that mirrors external conflict
  - Must be overcome (or not — in tragedy) for the character to transform

### The Four Dimensions

- **Physical**: How they look, move, dress — the body tells a story
- **Psychological**: How they think, what they fear, what they believe
- **Social**: How they relate to others, their role in community, their status
- **Philosophical**: What they believe about the world, their moral code, their worldview

## Example Profiles

### Lira Voss — The Cartographer's Daughter

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | Lira Voss |
| **Age** | 22 |
| **Role** | Protagonist |
| **Occupation** | Apprentice cartographer |
| **Motivation** | Complete her mother's unfinished map |
| **Fatal Flaw** | Compulsive need to understand and control |
| **Ghost/Wound** | Mother died pursuing the same obsession |
| **Arc** | Learns that some things must be experienced, not charted |

- **Voice**: Precise, observational, uses spatial metaphors ("I need to orient myself," "the edges of this don't match")
- **Habits**: Sketches when nervous, measures distances by eye, can't enter a room without mentally mapping exits
- **Contradiction**: Craves the unknown but fears what she can't categorize
- **Key Relationships**:
  - Kael: Challenges her worldview — he values mystery over knowledge
  - Master Aldric: Represents the safe path she's leaving behind
  - Her mother's memory: The ghost she's chasing and the warning she's ignoring

### Kael — The Guardian

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | Kael |
| **Age** | 25 |
| **Role** | Ally / Love Interest |
| **Occupation** | Border guardian of the Uncharted Land |
| **Motivation** | Protect his homeland from being mapped and claimed |
| **Fatal Flaw** | Distrust of outsiders, reflexive isolation |
| **Ghost/Wound** | Watched imperial cartographers destroy a neighboring territory by mapping it |
| **Arc** | Opens himself to connection beyond borders |

- **Voice**: Spare, direct, rarely wastes words — speaks in observations rather than opinions
- **Habits**: Moves silently, always aware of exits, sleeps lightly
- **Contradiction**: Protects a land defined by openness through closed, guarded behavior
- **Key Relationships**:
  - Lira: Forces him to consider that not all outsiders are conquerors
  - The Unmade: He serves beings he can never fully understand
  - The Surveyor: A dark mirror — someone who also believes borders define identity

### The Surveyor — The Antagonist

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | The Surveyor (real name withheld until Act III) |
| **Age** | 50s |
| **Role** | Antagonist |
| **Occupation** | Imperial Chief Cartographer |
| **Motivation** | Map the Uncharted Land to secure the empire's future |
| **Fatal Flaw** | Belief that knowledge equals ownership |
| **Ghost/Wound** | Rose from poverty through cartography — mapping was his escape, and he can't conceive of it as harmful |
| **Arc** | Refuses to change — a cautionary counterpoint to Lira |

- **Voice**: Elegant, persuasive, uses language as precisely as he uses a compass
- **Habits**: Never raises his voice, always polite, frames exploitation as progress
- **Contradiction**: Genuinely believes he's bringing order to chaos, which makes him more dangerous than a villain who knows he's evil
- **Key Relationships**:
  - Lira: Sees in her the talent he once had — wants to mentor her, recruit her, or destroy her
  - Thessaly Voss: Respected her, then watched her fail — views her failure as proof that his systematic approach is superior

## Character Development Checklist

For each major character, answer these questions:

- [ ] What do they want more than anything? (External goal)
- [ ] What do they actually need? (Internal growth)
- [ ] What is their fatal flaw?
- [ ] What wound created that flaw?
- [ ] How do they change by the end — or why don't they?
- [ ] Can a reader identify them by dialogue alone?
- [ ] Do they have contradictions that make them feel human?
- [ ] What is their relationship to the theme?
