# Key Experiments in the History of Science

The great breakthroughs in science are often anchored by a single decisive experiment — a moment when nature was asked a clear question and gave an unambiguous answer.

## Landmark Experiments

| Year | Experiment | Scientist(s) | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1609 | Telescopic observation of the heavens | Galileo Galilei | Discovered Jupiter's moons and Venus's phases; provided direct evidence for heliocentrism |
| 1687 | Prism dispersion of white light | Isaac Newton | Showed white light is composed of a spectrum of colors, founding the science of optics |
| 1798 | Cavendish experiment | Henry Cavendish | First measurement of the gravitational constant `G`; effectively "weighed the Earth" |
| 1801 | Double-slit experiment | Thomas Young | Demonstrated the wave nature of light through interference patterns |
| 1831 | Electromagnetic induction | Michael Faraday | Showed a changing magnetic field produces an electric current — the basis of generators and transformers |
| 1859 | Publication of *On the Origin of Species* | Charles Darwin | Presented the theory of evolution by natural selection with extensive evidence |
| 1887 | Michelson-Morley experiment | Albert Michelson, Edward Morley | Failed to detect the "luminiferous aether"; set the stage for special relativity |
| 1897 | Discovery of the electron | J.J. Thomson | Proved atoms are not indivisible; discovered the first subatomic particle using cathode rays |
| 1911 | Gold foil experiment | Ernest Rutherford | Discovered the atomic nucleus by firing alpha particles at thin gold foil |
| 1919 | Solar eclipse observation | Arthur Eddington | Confirmed general relativity by measuring the bending of starlight around the Sun |
| 1928 | Discovery of penicillin | Alexander Fleming | Noticed bacterial inhibition by a mold; launched the antibiotic revolution |
| 1952 | Hershey-Chase experiment | Alfred Hershey, Martha Chase | Confirmed DNA (not protein) is the genetic material |
| 1953 | Miller-Urey experiment | Stanley Miller, Harold Urey | Simulated early Earth conditions and produced amino acids — building blocks of life |
| 1964 | Discovery of cosmic microwave background | Arno Penzias, Robert Wilson | Detected the afterglow of the Big Bang; confirmed the Big Bang theory |
| 2012 | Higgs boson detection | ATLAS and CMS (CERN) | Confirmed the Higgs field, which gives particles mass — the last piece of the Standard Model |
| 2015 | Gravitational wave detection | LIGO collaboration | First direct observation of gravitational waves from merging black holes, confirming Einstein's 1915 prediction |

## Patterns in Great Experiments

- Many breakthroughs came from **unexpected results** — Rutherford didn't expect particles to bounce back; Fleming didn't intend to discover penicillin
- **Precision measurement** often opens new physics — Michelson-Morley, Cavendish, LIGO
- The simplest experiments can have the deepest implications — Young's double slit requires only a light source and two slits

What did the Michelson-Morley experiment (1887) attempt to detect, and what was the result?
***
It attempted to detect the **luminiferous aether** — the hypothetical medium through which light was thought to travel. The experiment **failed to detect any aether**, implying that light does not need a medium. This null result set the stage for Einstein's special relativity.
```

What did Rutherford's gold foil experiment (1911) reveal about the structure of the atom?
***
Most alpha particles passed straight through the gold foil, but a few bounced back at large angles. This showed that the atom is mostly empty space with a small, dense, positively charged **nucleus** at its center — overturning Thomson's "plum pudding" model.
```

What did Penzias and Wilson accidentally discover in 1964, and why was it significant?
***
They discovered the **cosmic microwave background (CMB)** — a faint, uniform microwave radiation filling all of space. It is the **afterglow of the Big Bang**, providing strong evidence that the universe began in a hot, dense state approximately 13.8 billion years ago.
