# Einstein and the Theory of Relativity

Albert Einstein's theories of relativity overthrew Newtonian physics at extreme scales — high speeds and strong gravitational fields. They revealed a universe stranger and more beautiful than anyone had imagined.

![Albert Einstein in 1921](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Albert_Einstein_Head.jpg/400px-Albert_Einstein_Head.jpg)

[General Relativity Explained — PBS Space Time](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AwhKZ3fd9JA)

## Albert Einstein (1879–1955)

- Born in Ulm, Germany; worked as a patent clerk in Bern, Switzerland
- **1905 — "Miracle Year" (Annus Mirabilis)**
	- Published four groundbreaking papers while still a patent clerk
	- Special relativity, the photoelectric effect (later won the Nobel Prize), Brownian motion, and mass-energy equivalence
- Published the general theory of relativity in 1915
- Fled Nazi Germany in 1933; spent the rest of his life at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton

## Special Relativity (1905)

- Two postulates
	- The laws of physics are the same in all inertial (non-accelerating) reference frames
	- The speed of light in a vacuum (`c ≈ 3 × 10⁸ m/s`) is the same for all observers
- Consequences
	- **Time dilation** — a moving clock ticks slower than a stationary one
	- **Length contraction** — objects shorten in the direction of motion at high speeds
	- **Relativity of simultaneity** — events simultaneous in one frame may not be in another
	- **Mass-energy equivalence** — `E = mc²`

### Classical vs. Relativistic Mechanics

| Concept | Classical (Newton) | Relativistic (Einstein) |
|---|---|---|
| Time | Absolute, flows the same everywhere | Relative — depends on velocity and gravity |
| Space | Fixed, unchanging backdrop | Warps and curves in the presence of mass |
| Mass | Constant regardless of speed | Effective mass increases as speed approaches `c` |
| Speed limit | None in principle | Nothing can exceed the speed of light |
| Energy-mass | Separate, unrelated | Equivalent: `E = mc²` |
| Gravity | Instantaneous force at a distance | Curvature of spacetime caused by mass-energy |

## General Relativity (1915)

- Extended special relativity to include **gravity** and **accelerating frames**
- Core idea: **mass and energy curve spacetime**, and objects follow the curvature
	- "Matter tells spacetime how to curve; spacetime tells matter how to move" — John Archibald Wheeler
- Key predictions (all confirmed)
	- **Gravitational lensing** — light bends around massive objects
	- **Gravitational time dilation** — clocks run slower in stronger gravitational fields
	- **Black holes** — regions where spacetime curvature becomes infinite
	- **Gravitational waves** — ripples in spacetime (detected by LIGO in 2015, a century after prediction)
- The Einstein field equations: `Gμν + Λgμν = (8πG/c⁴)Tμν`

## Key Equations

- Mass-energy equivalence: `E = mc²`
- Lorentz factor: `γ = 1 / √(1 - v²/c²)`
- Time dilation: `Δt' = γΔt`
- Length contraction: `L' = L / γ`

## Legacy

- GPS satellites must correct for both special and general relativistic effects — without it, positions would drift by ~10 km/day
- Underpins modern cosmology, including the Big Bang model and the expanding universe
- Opened the door to nuclear energy (and weapons) through `E = mc²`
