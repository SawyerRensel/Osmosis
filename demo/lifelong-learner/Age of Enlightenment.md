# The Age of Enlightenment (c. 1685–1815)

The Enlightenment was an intellectual movement that championed reason, individual liberty, and skepticism of authority. Its thinkers reshaped philosophy, politics, and science — and their ideas still underpin modern democracies.

![Voltaire portrait](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/D%27apr%C3%A8s_Maurice_Quentin_de_La_Tour%2C_Portrait_de_Voltaire%2C_d%C3%A9tail_du_visage_%28ch%C3%A2teau_de_Ferney%29.jpg/400px-D%27apr%C3%A8s_Maurice_Quentin_de_La_Tour%2C_Portrait_de_Voltaire%2C_d%C3%A9tail_du_visage_%28ch%C3%A2teau_de_Ferney%29.jpg)

## John Locke (1632–1704)

- Often called the "Father of Liberalism"
- Key works: *Two Treatises of Government*, *An Essay Concerning Human Understanding*
- Core ideas
	- **Tabula rasa** — the mind begins as a blank slate; all knowledge comes from experience
	- **Natural rights** — life, liberty, and property are inherent to every person
	- Government exists by the **consent of the governed**
	- The right to revolution when government violates natural rights
- Profoundly influenced the American and French Revolutions

## Voltaire (1694–1778)

- Born François-Marie Arouet; pen name "Voltaire"
- Champion of **freedom of speech** and **religious tolerance**
- Key works: *Candide*, *Letters on the English*
- Core ideas
	- Fierce critic of the Catholic Church and institutional dogma
	- Advocated for separation of church and state
	- Used satire and wit as weapons against injustice
	- Admired the English system of constitutional monarchy and civil liberties
- Spent years in exile for his provocative writings

## Jean-Jacques Rousseau (1712–1778)

- Key works: *The Social Contract*, *Emile*, *Discourse on Inequality*
- Core ideas
	- **"Man is born free, and everywhere he is in chains"**
	- The **social contract** — legitimate political authority rests on a covenant among citizens
	- The **general will** — laws should reflect what is best for the community as a whole
	- Civilization corrupts natural human goodness
	- Education should nurture a child's natural development, not impose rote learning
- Influenced both the French Revolution and Romantic movement

## Immanuel Kant (1724–1804)

- Key works: *Critique of Pure Reason*, *Groundwork of the Metaphysics of Morals*
- Core ideas
	- **"Sapere aude"** — "Dare to know!" (his motto for the Enlightenment)
	- The **categorical imperative** — act only according to maxims you could will to be universal laws
	- Synthesized rationalism and empiricism: the mind actively shapes experience
	- Morality must be grounded in reason, not tradition or emotion
	- Argued for perpetual peace through a federation of free states

## Common Threads

- Trust in **human reason** as the path to progress
- Skepticism of inherited authority — monarchy, church, tradition
- Belief in **individual rights** and the dignity of every person
- Optimism that society can be improved through knowledge
